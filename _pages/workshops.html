## GRAD-MAP Winter Workshop
Where and When? The 2024 GRAD-MAP Winter Workshop took place online (over Zoom) in January 2025.
Cost The Winter Workshop has no fee. Participants only need a stable internet connection.
Eligibility Any undergraduate student at a US institution interested in physics or astronomy and who supports the GRAD-MAP mission may apply. Preference is given to students from institutions without significant undergraduate research opportunities, including community colleges. Applications open at the beginning of November.
Professional Development
  • Seminars on graduate school, internships, and career paths in physics/astronomy.
  • Workshops on writing, presentations, and overcoming barriers in research access.
  • Facilitated panels and informal conversations throughout the workshop.
Scientific Computing with Python We use Anaconda and Jupyter notebooks to teach Python in a beginner-friendly format, adapted from the Goddard Python Bootcamp.
Research Projects Students complete mini-research projects under mentorship from UMD researchers. Topics include cosmology, astrophysics, planetary science, materials science, and more. Final presentations wrap up the workshop.
Tours of Scientific Facilities All 2024 tours were virtual. Past virtual tours include:
  • National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)
  • NASA Goddard Space Flight Center
  • Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory
  • National Radio Astronomy Observatory at Green Bank

## GRAD-MAP Summer Scholars
Program Overview Summer Scholars are selected from Winter Workshop alumni to participate in a 9-week paid research experience at UMD. It builds on the winter experience with full research projects, science identity development, and cohort support.
