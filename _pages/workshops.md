## GRAD-MAP Winter Workshop

<details>
<summary><strong>Where and When?</strong></summary>
<p>The 2024 GRAD-MAP Winter Workshop took place online (over Zoom) in January 2024.</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Cost</strong></summary>
<p>The Winter Workshop has no fee. Participants need only have internet connection for the duration of the workshop.</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Eligibility</strong></summary>
<p>
Any undergraduate student at a U.S. institution interested in physics or astronomy and who supports the GRAD-MAP mission may apply. Preference is given to students from institutions without significant research opportunities, including community colleges.  
Applications open at the beginning of November.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Professional Development</strong></summary>
<ul>
  <li>Practical, honest discussions about internships, grad school, and careers with a PhD.</li>
  <li>Workshops on essay writing, presentation skills, and navigating barriers.</li>
  <li>Facilitated panels and informal discussions during the workshop.</li>
</ul>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Scientific Computing with Python</strong></summary>
<p>
Interactive tutorials using Python, Jupyter notebooks, and the Anaconda distribution. These are adapted from the Goddard Python Bootcamp and designed for students new to coding.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Research Projects</strong></summary>
<p>
Participants work on mini-research projects with faculty and graduate mentors in fields like cosmology, stellar astrophysics, planetary science, and more.  
The workshop concludes with a student presentation session.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Tours of Scientific Facilities</strong></summary>
<p>
In 2024, all tours were virtual. Past facilities include:
</p>
<ul>
  <li>National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)</li>
  <li>NASA Goddard Space Flight Center (GSFC)</li>
  <li>Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory (APL)</li>
  <li>National Radio Astronomy Observatory at Green Bank</li>
</ul>
</details>

## GRAD-MAP Summer Scholars

<details>
<summary><strong>Program Overview</strong></summary>
<p>
GRAD-MAP Summer Scholars are selected from Winter Workshop alumni for a 10-week paid research program at UMD. The summer program builds on the winter experience with full research projects, cohort community, and science identity development.
</p>
</details>
