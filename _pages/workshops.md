## GRAD-MAP Winter Workshop

<details open>
  <summary><strong>Where and When?</strong></summary>
  <p>The 2024 GRAD-MAP Winter Workshop took place online (over Zoom) in January 2024.</p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><strong>Cost</strong></summary>
  <p>The Winter Workshop has no fee. Participants only need a stable internet connection for the duration of the workshop.</p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><strong>Eligibility</strong></summary>
  <p>
    Any undergraduate student at a US institution interested in graduate school or a career in physics or astronomy and who supports the GRAD-MAP mission may apply. 
    Preference is given to students from institutions without significant undergraduate research opportunities, including community colleges.
    <br><br>
    Applications open at the beginning of November.
  </p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><strong>Professional Development</strong></summary>
  <ul>
    <li>Practical, honest discussions about internships, graduate school, and career paths with a PhD in astronomy or physics.</li>
    <li>Skill-building in writing application essays and giving presentations.</li>
    <li>Addressing challenges faced by students with limited research access through seminars and informal conversations.</li>
  </ul>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><strong>Scientific Computing with Python</strong></summary>
  <p>
    Students learn Python through interactive tutorials using Jupyter notebooks and Anaconda. Sessions are adapted from the Goddard Python Bootcamp and geared toward beginners.
  </p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><strong>Research Projects</strong></summary>
  <p>
    Participants complete mini-research projects mentored by physics and astronomy researchers. Topics have included cosmology, stellar astrophysics, planetary science, materials science, and more.
    <br><br>
    Mentorship training draws on the University of Wisconsin's Research Mentoring resources. Students present their findings at the end of the workshop.
  </p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary><strong>Tours of Scientific Facilities</strong></summary>
  <p>All 2024 tours were virtual. Past tours included:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>NIST</li>
    <li>NASA Goddard Space Flight Center</li>
    <li>Johns Hopkins APL</li>
    <li>NRAO at Green Bank</li>
  </ul>
</details>
